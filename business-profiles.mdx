## What is a business profile?

A business profile is PostGlider's memory for one business location. Every piece of content you generate draws from the profile attached to your session — business name, location, services, target audience, tone, and the brand glossary you define.

You can have multiple profiles if you manage content for more than one business or location.

## Creating a profile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to My Profiles" icon="briefcase">
    From the sidebar, tap **My Profiles**. You'll see a list of your existing profiles and a **+ New profile** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the basics" icon="pen-line">
    Enter your business name, city and state, and pick your industry from the dropdown. If your industry isn't listed, choose **Other** and type it in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your audience and pitch" icon="users">
    Describe who your ideal customer is and what you want every post to accomplish. PostGlider uses this to angle every caption toward your actual audience — not a generic one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let PostGlider research your business" icon="search">
    If you have a website URL, enter it. PostGlider pulls your services, location signals, and any Google Business data it finds — so your first generated post is already specific to you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Don't overthink the audience and pitch fields. A sentence or two is enough. You can always edit them later from the profile detail page.
</Callout>

## Profile detail — what each section does

Navigate to a profile by clicking it in **My Profiles**. The detail page has several sections.

### Basic information

| Field | What it affects |
|---|---|
| **Business name** | Appears in content; used to match your Google Business listing |
| **Location** | City + state context in post captions |
| **Industry** | Used to filter Genie themes and tagging vocabulary |
| **Target audience** | Every caption is written with this person in mind |
| **Brand tone** | Sets the voice: Professional, Casual, Playful, Luxury, or Direct |
| **Services** | Specific offerings you want featured in content |
| **Differentiation** | What makes you different — used in promotional angles |

### Glossary

The glossary is a list of business-specific terms PostGlider should recognize and use correctly. Add things like:

- Branded service names (e.g., "The 90-Minute Reset" instead of "a massage")
- Staff names you want mentioned
- Customer nicknames or insider terms your audience will recognize
- Locations you serve that aren't in your main address

PostGlider automatically populates a starter glossary when it researches your business. You can add, edit, or remove entries at any time.

<Callout kind="info">
  Glossary terms also feed into the Media Vault auto-tagger — images get tagged with your specific vocabulary instead of generic category labels.
</Callout>

### Specialization codes

PostGlider runs a one-time AI classification to identify your NAICS and SIC industry codes. These codes are used internally to:

- Match you with content templates from similar businesses
- Improve the relevance of Genie theme suggestions
- Power the competitor intelligence feed

You'll see the detected codes on your profile page. You can confirm them if they're correct or run the classification again if the initial codes seem off.

### Competitor tracking

Add URLs for businesses you consider competitors. PostGlider monitors their public social content and surfaces intelligence nudges on your Content Studio — letting you know if a competitor posted something relevant so you can respond with your own angle.

You're not required to add competitors. It's an optional signal that improves theme suggestions over time.

### Google Business Profile

Paste your Google Business Profile URL here. This links your profile to your GMB listing so PostGlider can:

- Import your GMB photos directly into the Media Vault
- Pull in recent customer reviews as content inspiration
- Refresh your business data if your hours or services change

<Callout kind="tip">
  To find your Google Business Profile URL, search for your business on Google Maps, open your listing, and copy the URL from the address bar. It usually starts with `https://www.google.com/maps/place/...`
</Callout>

## Profile limits by tier

| Tier | Profiles |
|---|---|
| Free | 1 |
| Pro | 3 |
| Pro Plus | Unlimited |
| Enterprise | Unlimited |

If you hit your limit, upgrade from the **Billing** page or delete an unused profile.

## Deleting a profile

On the **My Profiles** page, click the trash icon on any profile card. You'll be asked to confirm. Deleting a profile removes the profile data but does **not** delete images in your Media Vault — those stay.

<Callout kind="danger">
  Profile deletion is permanent. All glossary terms, specialization codes, and competitor entries tied to that profile are removed immediately.
</Callout>
