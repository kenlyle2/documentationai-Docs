## Why your profile matters

PostGlider generates content from your profile — not a template. Every caption, call to action, and post angle is written for your specific business, audience, and voice. A complete profile means content that sounds like you wrote it.

This guide walks through completing a profile from scratch.

## Step 1: Create the profile

1. Go to **My Profiles** in the sidebar
2. Click **+ New profile**
3. Enter your business name and location (city, state)
4. Choose your industry from the dropdown

That's enough to start generating. Everything below makes the content better.

## Step 2: Import your business data

If you have a website, enter the URL and click **Import My Site**. PostGlider will:
- Pull your services and offerings
- Find your Google Business Profile if it exists
- Read customer review keywords and import them as glossary terms
- Pre-fill your location and industry if they're more specific than what you entered

<Callout kind="tip">
  If PostGlider finds your GMB listing, it will ask you to confirm it. Confirm it — this unlocks photo import and review-based content themes.
</Callout>

## Step 3: Describe your audience

In the **Target audience** field, describe the customer you're trying to reach. Be specific:

- "Homeowners in the Austin area with lawns larger than half an acre"
- "Women 35–55 who want to invest in self-care and have disposable income"
- "Small business owners who need professional headshots for LinkedIn"

Vague descriptions like "everyone" or "people who need my service" produce generic content. Specific descriptions produce content that speaks directly to your actual customer.

## Step 4: Write your pitch

The pitch fields tell PostGlider what you want every post to ultimately accomplish:

| Field | What to write |
|---|---|
| **Services promoted** | Your core offering — the one thing you most want customers to buy |
| **Differentiation** | Why you specifically — what competitors can't say |
| **Customer result** | The transformation you deliver — before vs. after, problem → solution |

Example for a landscaper:
- Services: "Weekly lawn maintenance and seasonal cleanup"
- Differentiation: "Family-owned, same crew every visit, 15-year customers are common"
- Customer result: "They go from embarrassed about their yard to being the neighbors everyone compliments"

## Step 5: Review and refine your glossary

PostGlider auto-populates a glossary from your website and reviews. Open the **Glossary** section of your profile and check what was generated.

Add or edit terms that are specific to your business:
- Branded service names ("The Spring Refresh Package")
- Staff names you want mentioned in content
- Neighborhood names or area references your customers use
- Industry terms your audience knows and uses

Remove any terms that feel generic or wrong.

## Step 6: Confirm your specialization codes

PostGlider runs a background classification to identify your industry codes (NAICS/SIC). These improve Genie theme suggestions over time. When the classification is ready, you'll see a notification on your profile page. Review and confirm the suggested codes.

## Step 7: Connect your Google Business Profile

In the **Google Business Profile** section, paste your GMB URL. PostGlider will import your existing GMB photos and refresh them automatically when you run an enrichment.

<Callout kind="tip">
  To find your GMB URL, search your business name on Google Maps, click your listing, and copy the URL from the address bar.
</Callout>

## Your profile is ready

Once you've completed steps 1–4, you can generate your first content. Steps 5–7 improve quality over time but aren't required to get started.

Return to your profile anytime to update your audience description, add new glossary terms, or refresh your services. PostGlider picks up changes immediately on the next generation.
